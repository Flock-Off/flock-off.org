The Problem

Flock Safety is building a
nationwide tracking network.

Flock Safety sells Automatic License Plate Reader (ALPR) cameras to police departments, sheriff's offices, and HOAs across the country — logging every vehicle that passes, with no warrant, no suspicion, and no oversight.

Every Plate. Every Pass. Logged.

Flock cameras don't wait for a crime. They photograph and record every vehicle that drives by — permanently building a database of where you go, when you go there, and how often.

Nationwide AI Surveillance

These systems don't operate in isolation. AI-powered networks link agencies across the country, turning local camera installations into a nationwide tracking infrastructure — your movements analyzed, stored, and shared without your knowledge.

No Warrant. No Suspicion. No Problem.

Courts have ruled license plates in public carry no expectation of privacy. Flock exploits this loophole to track your every movement without a judge ever signing off — the Fourth Amendment bypassed by geography.

HOAs as Surveillance Proxies

Flock aggressively markets to homeowners associations and private neighborhoods, creating civilian-run surveillance networks that feed data directly to law enforcement — no public vote, no oversight, no accountability.

You Cannot Opt Out

You can clear your browser history, use a VPN, and delete your apps. You cannot stop a Flock camera from scanning your plate. If you drive on a public road, you are being tracked.

No Rules on Retention or Access

There is no federal law governing how long Flock data can be kept, who can query it, or what it can be used for. Departments set their own policies — and most have none at all.
