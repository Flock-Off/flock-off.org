Chapters

Bring the fight to your campus

Flock Off chapters organize students and educators to push back against surveillance technology in schools and universities across the country.

High Schools

Students in K–12 schools are among the most surveilled populations in the country. Flock Off high school chapters give students a platform to fight license plate readers, facial recognition, and invasive monitoring tools installed on campus.

Know Your Rights

Learn what surveillance tools are being used in your school and what legal protections apply to you as a student.

Petition Your School Board

We provide templates and guidance for presenting your case to administrators and elected school board members.

Build Your Chapter

Recruit fellow students, connect with local advocacy groups, and coordinate action with chapters across the country.

Start a High School Chapter

No experience needed — just a willingness to organize. We'll walk you through every step.

Colleges & Universities

Universities are rapidly expanding surveillance infrastructure — from smart cameras to predictive policing software. College chapters lead research, advocacy, and direct organizing on campuses nationwide.

Campus Research

File public records requests and investigate what surveillance contracts your university has signed — and with whom.

Faculty & Admin Engagement

Work with sympathetic faculty to bring formal resolutions and policy debates to faculty senates and student governments.

Coalition Building

Partner with other student organizations — civil liberties, privacy, racial justice — to build a broad coalition on campus.

Start a College Chapter

Join a national network of students holding their institutions accountable.
